ANSWER KEY SUMMARY (UPDATED)

Note: Complete detailed answers for all questions are available in the separate Answer Key document. Below is a summary of key concepts to check:

Section 1: Basic Concepts

  • Light is energy that travels in straight lines
  • Natural sources: Sun, stars, fireflies
  • Artificial sources: bulbs, candles, torches

Section 2: Reflection

  • Reflection = light bouncing off surfaces
  • Incident ray → hits surface
  • Reflected ray → bounces back
  • Virtual image in mirrors: upright, same size, laterally inverted

Section 3: Laws of Reflection

  • Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
  • Both measured from the normal
  • Angles lie in the same plane

Section 4: Types of Reflection

  • Specular: smooth surfaces, clear images
  • Diffuse: rough surfaces, scattered light

Section 5: Plane Mirrors

  • Image: virtual, upright, same size, laterally inverted
  • Image distance = Object distance
  • Periscopes use two plane mirrors

Section 6: Refraction

  • Bending of light when changing medium
  • Air → Glass: bends toward normal (slows down)
  • Glass → Air: bends away from normal (speeds up)
  • No bending if perpendicular to surface

Section 7: Applications

  • Periscopes: see over obstacles
  • Lenses: refract light for vision correction
  • Prisms: separate white light into colors
  • Fiber optics: total internal reflection

Section 8: Ray Diagrams

  • Use ruler for straight lines
  • Include arrows for direction
  • Label all parts
  • Show normal as dashed line perpendicular to surface

Section 9: Materials

  • Transparent: clear passage (glass, water)
  • Translucent: partial passage (frosted glass, thin cloth)
  • Opaque: blocks light (wood, metal)

Section 10: Calculations

  • If angle of incidence = 30°, angle of reflection = 30°
  • If object is 15 cm from mirror, image is 15 cm behind
  • Distance between object and image = 2 × object distance

Section 16: LENSES (NEW)

  • Convex lens: Thicker in middle, converges light, forms both real and virtual images
  • Concave lens: Thinner in middle, diverges light, always forms virtual diminished images
  • Focal point: Point where parallel rays converge (convex) or appear to diverge from (concave)
  • Focal length: Distance from center of lens to focal point
  • Real images: Can be projected, inverted, formed by convex lens when object beyond F
  • Virtual images: Cannot be projected, upright, formed by convex lens when object between F and lens, always by concave lens
  • Applications: Magnifying glass (convex), cameras (convex), eyeglasses (both types), microscopes (convex), correcting vision defects
